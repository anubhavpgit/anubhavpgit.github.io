Hi, I am Anubhab Patnaik, a CS undergrad from the Silicon Institute of Technology, Bhubaneswar, India. Interested in math, science, and engineering, I work with software engineering and try to solve real-world problems.

I work with whatever piques my interest. I used to dabble in ML and now work with full-stack web and mobile applications. I polish my DS and algo on Leetcode. Lately, I've been working on a custom bit-torrent client to download movies based on recommendations from my movie recommendation system and a movie rating prediction system. I'm planning to port it to Rust later. I also built a school/college resource-sharing website called resoc which is under active development in cbrtl. I am also modifying my custom SSG, zuzu, to port my existing blog, and implement a search feature. Meanwhile, I am also exploring esoteric programming languages and will be working on an brainfuck interpreter.

When I'm not coding up a storm, I engage with my FOSS communities. I am the co-founder of the official coding club in my college, SWITCH, founded by Saurav Jha. We focus on establishing a coding community for students by conducting workshops, hackathons, and coding competitions. We also provide a platform for students to showcase their skills and get recognition for their work. Srijan introduced me to cbrtl, a small, tight-knit group of programmers and open-source sorcerers who like to craft fun projects together and build a community. We are a group of developers and primarily hang out on GitHub. I was the Student Chairperson(2022-2023) of the prestigious IEEE Students' Chapter, Silcion Institute. We conduct workshops, hackathons, and coding competitions on a larger scale.
